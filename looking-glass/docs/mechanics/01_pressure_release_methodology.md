# Pressure Release Methodology

## Purpose

This document describes the mechanical pattern AsymX uses to work with operational friction in organizations.

It is not a consultancy method.

It is not a diagnosis tool.

It is not therapy.

It is not an audit.

It is a visibility technique.

The goal is to make operational pressure findable, reviewable and releasable without blame.

---

## Core Principle

Massage does not force the body to relax.

Massage makes tension findable, touchable and releasable.

AsymX does not force an organization to change.

AsymX makes operational friction visible enough to review, route and release without blame.

Core rule:

> Wat zichtbaar genoeg is, hoeft nu niet opnieuw gedragen te worden.  
> Wat leeft, krijgt ruimte om te groeien.

---

## 1. Pressure In A Living System

In a body, pressure can become tension.

In an organization, pressure can become operational friction.

When pressure stays invisible, people carry it.

When pressure becomes visible enough, it can be reviewed, routed, softened or released.

AsymX treats friction as a visible signal, not as blame.

The goal is not to prove that something is wrong.

The goal is to make the pressure point visible enough so it no longer has to be silently carried.

---

## 2. The Five Phases

### Phase 1 — Contact

First gentle contact with the field.

No pressure.

No judgement.

Only: what is visible?

AsymX movement:

public signal  
to visibility question  
to claim boundary

---

### Phase 2 — Palpation

Locate the points where pressure may be concentrated.

This can include:

- repeated locations
- repeated items
- repeated time windows
- repeated corrections
- repeated planning gaps
- repeated visibility delays
- repeated unclear ownership

At this phase, repetition is not proof.

Repetition is only a signal that something may be reviewable.

---

### Phase 3 — Pressure

Apply only enough pressure to make the signal clearer.

In AsymX, pressure means a small read-only validation pattern.

Examples:

- count repetition
- compare planned signal with observed signal
- identify missing fields
- mark visibility lag
- separate public source from interpretation
- route an open question

Pressure must stay reversible.

Pressure must not become diagnosis.

---

### Phase 4 — Release

Release happens when the pressure point becomes safe enough to move.

This may mean:

- the question becomes clear
- the signal is routed
- the uncertainty is parked
- the source boundary is improved
- the claim is softened
- the next safe step becomes visible
- blame leaves the field

Release is not a forced conclusion.

Release is the moment where the system no longer needs to carry the same pressure silently.

---

### Phase 5 — Maintenance

After release, the movement remains traceable.

Maintenance happens through:

- movement logs
- status checks
- routing labels
- claim boundaries
- parked topics
- versioned files
- visible commits
- review before formal output

Maintenance prevents invisible pressure from returning as hidden work.
