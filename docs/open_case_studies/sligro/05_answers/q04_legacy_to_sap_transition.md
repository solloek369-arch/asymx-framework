# Case 05 Answer Q04 — Legacy to SAP Transition

## Question

What typically becomes difficult when a company moves from legacy ERP logic to standardised ERP processes?

## Domain

ERP transition / legacy systems / masterdata / process harmonisation / operational visibility

## Short answer

When an organisation moves from legacy ERP logic to standardised ERP processes, the difficult part is often not only the software change. The harder transition is translating years of locally evolved workflows, custom logic, workarounds, implicit knowledge and data assumptions into cleaner definitions, harmonised processes and stronger masterdata discipline. This can create a temporary visibility gap between historical floor reality and the new standard process language. In claim-safe wording: this is a general ERP transition pattern, not a claim about Sligro internal operations.

## What the sources support

The Computable article supports that Sligro publicly moved from a self-built ERP application on IBM AS/400 toward SAP S/4HANA for Retail, and that this fits a broader movement from self-build and in-house control toward standard applications and a regie-oriented IT model.

The Sligro half-year 2025 presentation supports that the ERP transition is publicly connected to integrated ERP design, preparation of organisation and masterdata, SAP configuration and a phased rollout.

Together, these sources support the public context of a large legacy-to-standard ERP transition. They do not prove any internal floor problem, data issue, delay or migration defect.

## Claim-safe wording

A transition from historical legacy ERP logic to a standardised ERP environment often requires organisations to translate locally evolved workflows, data assumptions and implicit operational routines into harmonised process definitions. In that transition, it can be useful to keep small floor-level signals visible, so the organisation can see where historical working reality and the new standard process language still need alignment.

## AsymX Level 1 translation

A suitable AsymX Level 1 lens is a local Transition Signal Lens.

The lens captures repeated local transition signals as metadata, for example:

- workarounds;
- correction patterns;
- scan exceptions;
- execution notes;
- repeated location or process-step friction;
- floor observations during process harmonisation.

Level 1 boundaries:

- local only;
- browser-only where possible;
- no backend;
- no upload;
- no tracking;
- source data stays intact;
- analysis is metadata only;
- no writeback to SAP, AS/400, WMS, planning or BI;
- no replacement of existing systems.

The purpose is not to prove error. The purpose is to make visible where transition knowledge is still being carried by people, process or local context.
