## Publicly visible

Sligro is publicly visible as a large foodservice operation with logistics, planning, delivery, service level, supply chain integration and central distribution as important operational themes.

The Management Scope interview frames the logistics challenge in practical language: suppliers, internal organisation and transport to the customer.

Public Sligro and partner sources also show a complex planning environment with a broad product range, many SKUs, central distribution, delivery services, integration work and service-level attention.

## Simple question

How can a logistics organisation keep the operational challenge simple and reliable while systems, assortment, planning, delivery and execution become more complex?

In AsymX language:

Where does the system image need a small local visibility layer so that floor reality, planning and availability can be compared without creating another heavy IT project?

## Possible pattern

This case points to a general sector pattern, not a Sligro-specific diagnosis.

Central planning and ERP or WMS-style system images can provide overview, but local floor reality still moves in time, location, people, availability, scan moments, exceptions and recovery work.

The possible pattern is therefore not system failure, but a visibility interval:

- planning sees expectation;
- the floor sees momentary reality;
- service level depends on the timing between both;
- employees often absorb small mismatches before they become visible in dashboards.

## AsymX Level 1 answer

A fitting AsymX Level 1 response is a local Logistics Shadow Run Lens.

The lens does not replace SAP, Slim4, WMS or any operational system.

It only makes a small local question visible:

Where do repeated signals, delays, mismatches, missing items, scan or update moments, or recovery actions cluster?

Level 1 rules:

- local only;
- no backend;
- no upload;
- no tracking;
- no external dependencies unless explicitly chosen;
- source stays intact;
- analysis remains metadata;
- no hard claims without measurement.

## What this does not claim

This open case study does not claim:

- that Sligro has a specific internal defect;
- that SAP, Slim4, WMS, AS400, ABS or any other system is wrong;
- that there is an internal crisis;
- that public sources prove ghost picks, manco patterns or floor-system mismatch inside Sligro;
- that AsymX has measured Sligro operations;
- that AsymX is a complete supply-chain solution.

This is an external, public, claim-safe visibility proposal.

## Mail value

This case is valuable for the eventual Sligro mail because it connects to Sligro public language:

- keep logistics practical;
- do not make the challenge heavier than necessary;
- support service level and operational reliability;
- respect existing systems;
- place the employee and floor reality inside the visibility loop.

Possible mail line:

The invitation is not to add another large IT layer, but to test one small local visibility lens where planning, system image and floor reality can be compared safely.

## Invitation

The goal would not be to prove blame.
