# Change Process — Public Source Reviews

## Purpose

This document records why this open-source case layer changes over time.

The goal is not to pretend that every first version is perfect.

The goal is to make the movement visible.

When we change direction, we document:

1. what we were looking at first;
2. what became visible;
3. why we changed;
4. what the new middle position is;
5. what remains open for feedback.

## Current Context

This repository contains open-source AsymX visibility work.

Some documents refer to publicly visible information about real companies, including Sligro.

That creates a responsibility.

Public information may be used, but only when the source status, interpretation layer and claim boundary remain clearly separated.

## Initial Direction

At first, the work moved toward an open case-study structure.

The intention was:

- to use public information;
- to show how AsymX reads visible patterns;
- to create a bridge from public signal to safe question;
- to avoid internal claims;
- to avoid diagnosis;
- to avoid blaming any company, system or people.

This direction was useful because it made the structure concrete.

But it also made something else visible.

## What Became Visible

Even when individual sentences are claim-safe, the structure around them can still create risk.

Risk can appear in:

- folder names;
- document titles;
- commit messages;
- case-study framing;
- missing source-status blocks;
- unclear separation between public source and AsymX interpretation.

This showed that claim safety is not only a writing issue.

It is also an architecture issue.

## The Other Extreme

One possible reaction was to move fully to damage-control mode:

- remove company names;
- rewrite history;
- hide the movement;
- treat public information as if it were private;
- frame the correction as a mistake.

That also did not feel right.

Public information is not automatically private information.

Open-source visibility work is allowed when it is careful, transparent and clearly bounded.

## The Middle Position

We are now moving to the middle.

The middle position is:

- do not hide;
- do not overclaim;
- do not diagnose;
- do not pretend internal access;
- do not treat public information as private;
- do make source status visible;
- do separate public source from AsymX interpretation;
- do show claim boundaries;
- do invite correction;
- do keep the process open.

This is the current working position.

## AC Mix Model

The AC Mix model is used here as a balancing layer.

It helps prevent movement toward either extreme:

- too open without enough claim boundaries;
- too closed out of fear or damage-control thinking.

The AC Mix position asks:

1. What is actually public?
2. What are we adding as interpretation?
3. What must remain unclaimed?
4. What needs feedback?
5. What should stay open?

The model does not say that the current version is perfect.

It only helps us keep the movement visible, balanced and correctable.

## Current Rule

If a public company name is used, the document must make clear:

- the information is publicly visible;
- no internal data is used;
- no internal truth is claimed;
- the AsymX layer is interpretation, not proof;
- any next step is an invitation to review, not a conclusion.

## Feedback Invitation

If someone sees a problem with this framing, we want to hear it.

The intent is to do this as carefully and respectfully as possible.

We may not know immediately whether we have done that well enough.

If something turns out not to be good enough, that is not hidden.

It becomes information for improving the process.

## Ongoing Process

This is an ongoing process.

AsymX does not assume that the first structure is final.

The movement itself is part of the work.

When something becomes visible, we adjust.

When the adjustment is meaningful, we document why.

## Closing Rule

We try to do this as well as we can.

We make the reason for movement visible.

If it can be improved, that improvement becomes part of the system.
