# External Mirror Bundling

## Status

Public mechanism note.

This document describes how AsymX handles external people, projects, forks, signals, or research lines that are already active in a theme AsymX touches.

It does not claim ownership.
It does not claim proof.
It does not recruit.
It does not create hierarchy.

## Core Pattern

Sometimes an external signal appears around a theme that AsymX is also exploring.

The signal is not treated as confirmation by itself.

It is treated as a mirror.

```text
external signal
mirror
re-check
temporary expert gate
possible bundling
continued freedom
```

## Mirror

A mirror reflects overlap in theme, not authority over the other line of work.

Theme overlap means energy may already be moving elsewhere. That is information for coordination, not a scorecard.

## Re-check

After mirroring, the work is re-checked against the current public pattern: what is visible, what is parked, and what still needs a human gate before any shared or amplified use.

Re-check is mechanical: compare traces, not reputations.

## Temporary expert gate

When bundling could reduce duplication or make collaboration more efficient, a **temporary expert gate** is the narrow decision moment:

- Is the overlap real in mechanism terms (not in hype)?
- Is combining effort actually lighter for everyone involved?
- Can each party keep their own fork, timeline, and credit semantics intact?

The gate is temporary: it exists for a bounded decision, not as a standing role over people or projects.

## Possible bundling

**Bundling** here means optional alignment of compatible public pieces (interfaces, vocabulary, checkpoints) when parties freely choose it.

Bundling is invitation-based. It is efficient collaboration when it fits; it is also fine when parties stay parallel without merging paths.

## No ownership, no pressure

No party is asked to surrender ownership of their work, direction, or narrative.

No pressure to join, to prove alignment, or to stay after a bundling experiment ends.

If collaboration stops, traces can still meet traces later without conflict of claim.

## Trace meeting trace

When two public traces intersect in a theme, the meeting is read as structural overlap and optional coordination — not as proof that one side “validates” the other.

Trace meets trace; humans stay free to correct, fork, or walk away.

## Use of this note

This mechanism is for public, mechanism-first dialogue.

If you see theme overlap with something AsymX is running in the open, you can mirror it back, pass through a temporary expert gate if useful, and consider bundling only where it stays light, reversible, and respectful of independence.
