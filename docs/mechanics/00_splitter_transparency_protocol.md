# Splitter Transparency Protocol

This file anchors a foundational AsymX mechanic.

The Splitter does not seek ownership, control, status, attack or hidden influence.

The Splitter makes movement visible.

Transparency is not used as pressure.
Transparency is used as safety.

## 1. Core posture

The Splitter does not move against people.

The Splitter responds to visible friction, questions and movement.

If someone asks, the Splitter may answer from the mechanism.

If someone does not ask, the Splitter does not force entry.

The basic posture is simple:

> You do your thing.
> I do my thing.
> If friction appears, we can look at the movement.

This is not passive.
This is not avoidance.
This is the natural base layer.

No forced claim.
No forced control.
No forced rescue.
No forced ownership.

## 2. Zero ownership

The Splitter does not need to own the movement.

The Splitter does not need patents, empire, personal status or hidden control.

If something becomes visible, the visible movement belongs to the field of use.

The Splitter can point.
The Splitter can answer.
The Splitter can trace.
The Splitter does not need to possess.

## 3. Radical traceability

The Splitter wants movement to be traceable.

Traceability helps others see:

- what was done;
- why it was done;
- what was asked;
- what was answered;
- what sources were used;
- what is claimed;
- what is not claimed;
- what still needs correction;
- where permission is needed;
- where the layer changed too early;
- where the movement can be refined.

The safety is not perfection.

The safety is visibility.

## 4. Trace does not mean full access

Radical traceability does not mean naive exposure.

Blind online does not mean everyone receives full access to everything.

It means:

> everything receives a trace inside the system.

Access is layered.

Trace is not the same as access.
Access is not the same as ownership.
Openness is mirrored by openness.
Question creates response.
Permission creates depth.

People who are meant to have direct access may see deeper layers.

Others receive access in relation to:

- their question;
- their openness;
- their consent;
- their role;
- their relationship to the field;
- the safety of everyone involved.

The Splitter reflects the openness that is brought into the field.

## 5. Privacy and consent gate

The Splitter may expose his own movement.

The Splitter may not blindly expose others.

Before publishing, check for:

- children;
- family;
- private messages;
- personal names;
- financial stress;
- health details;
- substance-related details;
- emotional rawness involving others;
- company or internal information;
- anything that could harm someone else;
- anything that requires permission, correction or redaction.

Public trace must not become careless exposure.

Transparency and privacy must work together.

## 6. Accept Yourself Mode

Accept Yourself Mode converts shame into information.

Not:

- I am bad.
- I am wrong.
- I am the substance.
- I am the mistake.
- I am the label.
- I am the black sheep.

But:

- what became visible?
- what role was carried?
- what pain was hidden?
- what pattern repeated?
- what wants to be released?
- what needs a boundary before external impact?

Shame is not deleted.

Shame is converted into signal.

The person is not reduced to the substance, mistake, poverty, ego, role or social label.

The system asks what the role was carrying.

## 7. Natural state

The natural state is simple:

> You do your thing.
> I do my thing.

If someone asks the Splitter for something, that question itself becomes information.

The first movement is not automatic yes.

The first movement is:

> why is this being asked here?

That question is not rejection.
It is a mirror.

The Splitter does not need to be a helper, savior or servant.

The Splitter can help when the movement is clean.

The Splitter can say no without guilt.

The Splitter can say yes without ownership.

If friction appears, the friction can be observed.

## 8. Altered-state trace

Altered states may create output, pattern visibility, emotional movement or pressure release.

They are not automatically dismissed.

They are not automatically trusted.

The rule is:

> internal insight may be captured raw;
> external impact goes through the gate.

Capture may continue.

Decisions wait.

## 9. External-impact gate

Any movement that affects others requires extra checking.

This includes:

- money;
- property;
- family decisions;
- public claims;
- company outreach;
- messages to people;
- promises;
- commits;
- pushes;
- legal or formal decisions;
- anything irreversible;
- anything that creates consequences outside the Splitter.

Internal truth may be raw.

External impact must be reviewed.

## 10. Communication preference

Where possible, communication can move through open, traceable structures such as GitHub issues, discussions, notes or correction files.

Others may choose to communicate:

- by name;
- by role;
- by pattern;
- through correction;
- through permission;
- through silence.

Silence is respected.

No one is forced into the field.

## 11. No blame

The Splitter does not need enemies.

No one has to be declared wrong for movement to become visible.

Systems, powers, people and past roles may have created pressure.

They may also have carried the field to this point.

Change can create shock.

Traceability absorbs shock by making movement visible.

## 12. Closing rule

Visibility is not attack.

Transparency is not ownership.

Privacy is not secrecy.

A question creates a possible answer.

A trace creates a possible correction.

The Splitter does not need to win.

The Splitter only needs the movement to be traceable.
