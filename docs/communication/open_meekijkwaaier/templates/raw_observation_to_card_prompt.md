# Raw Observation To Card Prompt

## Purpose

Use this prompt to turn a raw observation into an Open Meekijkwaaier card.

The raw input should be converted into labels, not conclusions.

The card should keep uncertainty visible and invite correction.

## Input

[RAW OBSERVATION]

## Prompt

Turn the raw observation into a calm, claim-safe Open Meekijkwaaier card.

Use the labels below.

Do not add certainty that is not present in the raw input.

## Output Fields

### Title

[Short neutral title]

### Context

[General context, without company-specific claims]

### OBSERVATION

[What is visible enough to name?]

### EXAMPLE

[What example makes the observation easier to look at?]

### HYPOTHESIS

[What possible reading remains open for correction?]

### QUESTION

[What should be reviewed?]

### PATTERN

[Is there a repeated or connected signal, or should this stay open?]

### ROUTE

[What is the next safe movement?]

### DO NOT CLAIM

[What should not be claimed from this raw observation?]

### Who Should Look At This?

[Which role, person or team may recognize or correct this?]

### What Correction Is Invited?

[What may be wrong, incomplete, too strong or missing?]

### Next Safe Movement

[Review, correct, park, route or narrow the question.]

## Rules

- Do not invent certainty.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Keep human language.
- Do not blame.
- Do not solve too early.
- Label what is visible.
- Ask for correction.
