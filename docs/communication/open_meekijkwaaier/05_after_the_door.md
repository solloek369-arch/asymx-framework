# After The Door

## Purpose

This document describes what someone sees after entering the Open Meekijkwaaier.

The door is not the value.

After the door, a person enters a quiet entry room.

The entry room makes the first movement understandable without live explanation.

## Entry Questions

The entry room answers:

1. Where am I?
2. How do I look with this?
3. What can I safely do?

## Door Mat Sentence

> You do not need to believe this. You only need to look with it: what is recognizable, what is incorrect, and what can become sharper?

## Core Flow

Door -> Entry Room -> First Visible Object -> Role Entry -> Claim Status -> Correction -> Next Safe Movement

## Why The Entry Room Exists

The visitor should not enter the user's head.

The visitor should enter a calm shared room with one visible object on the table.

That object can be an observation, example, question, pattern or route.

It stays open for correction.

## Five Zones

### 1. Welcome / Pressure Release

The first zone lowers pressure.

It says what the room is and what it is not.

It keeps the invitation calm.

### 2. First Visible Object

The second zone places one visible object on the table.

The object is not a conclusion.

It is something people can look at together.

### 3. Role Entries

The third zone gives different roles a safe way in.

Each role can see, correct or complete a different part.

No role is asked to carry the whole field alone.

### 4. Correction Area

The fourth zone invites correction.

Correction can mean:

- recognizable
- incorrect
- missing
- unclear
- too strong
- needs parking

### 5. Next Safe Movement

The fifth zone names the next movement.

The movement can stay small.

It can be review, correction, parking, routing or a sharper question.

## Closing Rule

The door opens the room.

The room makes shared looking possible.

The next safe movement stays visible.
