## What bol.com publicly appears to have solved

*(Bullets summarise **only** what Redwood’s public page states or clearly attributes; wording is paraphrased.)*

- Pursuing **coordination** across supply-chain-related processes amid expansion — framed on the page around inventory complexity, **new suppliers**, and **downstream logistics and fulfilment**.
- Aiming for capabilities described on the page such as **next-day delivery**, **order tracking**, and **accurate forecasts** (as stated in the “Business Challenge” framing).
- Handling **high-volume, heterogeneous partner product information** with differing update cadences — the **Business Challenge** section attributes to bol.com discussion of partners sending product information **from every 10 minutes to every hour**, requiring timely extraction for site accuracy (**quote-level detail only on `R1`**).

- Using Redwood’s described capabilities to **automatically connect and orchestrate** facets of supply-chain processes; **modular** automation with **reuse** of definitions; emphasis on **consistency** and **fewer errors**; **event-driven** behaviour associated with **timely / accurate data** (Solution section paraphrase).
- Operational flows described at high level: ensuring **supplier information** is **extracted** and sent in an appropriate shape toward the **online catalogue**; **orders** checked against **stock** and **delivery times**; orders **passed toward logistics** (Solution section themes — **process summary only**, not technical architecture truth).
