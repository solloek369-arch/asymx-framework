## Publicly visible

Sligro and Slimstock publicly describe Slim4 as a long-used supply chain planning and replenishment layer.

Public Slimstock sources describe themes such as availability, efficiency, insight into ordering behaviour, supply chain planning, replenishment and stock reduction.

Public Sligro sources describe service level, delivery time, working capital, SAP preparation, masterdata preparation and further rollout of replenishment tooling as operationally relevant themes.

Public vacancy language shows that supply chain planning is not only analytical, but also connected to execution, locations, go-lives and colleagues in the field.

## Simple question

How can central planning and replenishment stay practically useful when physical availability, execution timing and local exceptions happen on the floor?

In AsymX language:

Where can a small local visibility layer help compare planning intention with momentary floor reality without replacing Slim4 or adding a heavy IT project?

## Possible pattern

This case points to a general sector pattern, not a Sligro-specific diagnosis.

Planning tools can help balance demand, stock, availability, service level and working capital.

The floor still moves through physical time:

- goods arrive;
- goods are stored;
- goods are picked;
- goods are delayed;
- goods are damaged;
- goods are moved;
- goods are scanned;
- goods are corrected;
- people absorb small mismatches before dashboards show them.

The possible pattern is therefore not a failure of planning.

The possible pattern is a planning-reality interval:

- planning sees expectation;
- replenishment sees needed movement;
- the floor sees momentary availability;
- service level depends on how quickly these views are synchronized.

## AsymX Level 1 answer

A fitting AsymX Level 1 response is a local Logistics Shadow Run Lens next to Slim4.

The lens does not replace Slim4.

The lens does not connect to internal Slimstock algorithms.

The lens does not judge planners or systems.

It only makes a small local question visible:

Where do planning intention, physical availability, scan or update moments and local recovery actions temporarily diverge?

Level 1 rules:

- local only;
- no backend;
- no upload;
- no tracking;
- no external dependencies unless explicitly chosen;
- source stays intact;
- analysis remains metadata;
- no hard claims without measurement.

## What this does not claim

This open case study does not claim:

- that Slim4 fails;
- that Sligro has planning problems;
- that planners are missing something;
- that SAP, Slim4, WMS, AS400, ABS or any other system is wrong;
- that public sources prove floor-system mismatch inside Sligro;
- that AsymX has measured Sligro operations;
- that AsymX is a replacement for supply chain planning software.

This is an external, public, claim-safe visibility proposal.
