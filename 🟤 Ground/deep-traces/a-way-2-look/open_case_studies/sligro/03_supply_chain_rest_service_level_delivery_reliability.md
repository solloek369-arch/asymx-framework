## Publicly visible

Sligro publicly reports good performance in service level, on-time delivery and customer satisfaction, partly realised through the rollout of the Slim4 replenishment tool in delivery service.

Sligro also publicly describes Belgium as a transition and recovery context, including network optimisation, regional delivery, restored calm in the operation and good and stable service levels.

Public sources therefore show service level, delivery reliability, customer satisfaction, route optimisation, regional delivery, working capital, network optimisation and operational calm as relevant themes.

## Simple question

How can service level, delivery reliability and customer satisfaction remain stable when supply chain costs, transition, planning and daily execution all move at the same time?

In AsymX language:

Where can a small local visibility layer help show repeated friction early enough, before it has to be absorbed by people, service recovery or extra operational movement?

## Possible pattern

This case points to a general sector pattern, not a Sligro-specific diagnosis.

After logistics integration, network optimisation or system transition, reported output can improve while local execution still depends on timing, people, routing, stock availability, customer agreements and exception handling.

The possible pattern is therefore not service failure.

The possible pattern is a stabilisation interval:

- the customer sees delivery reliability;
- management sees service level and customer satisfaction;
- planning sees route, stock and replenishment signals;
- the floor and delivery operation may still carry small local exceptions;
- operational calm depends on how quickly repeated friction becomes visible.

## AsymX Level 1 answer

A fitting AsymX Level 1 response is local visibility around repeated operational friction.

The lens does not solve the whole supply chain.

The lens does not replace Slim4, SAP, WMS, routing systems or existing performance dashboards.

It only makes a small local question visible:

Where do repeated delays, exceptions, recovery actions, timing issues or handover frictions cluster before they affect service level or have to be manually absorbed?

Level 1 rules:

- local only;
- no backend;
- no upload;
- no tracking;
- no external dependencies unless explicitly chosen;
- source stays intact;
- analysis remains metadata;
- no hard claims without measurement.

## What this does not claim

This open case study does not claim:

- that Sligro has service problems;
- that the Belgium transition failed;
- that Slim4, SAP, WMS, AS400, ABS or any other system is wrong;
- that service level, delivery reliability or customer satisfaction are weak;
- that public sources prove internal floor-system mismatch;
- that AsymX has measured Sligro operations;
- that AsymX is a complete supply-chain solution.

This is an external, public, claim-safe visibility proposal.
