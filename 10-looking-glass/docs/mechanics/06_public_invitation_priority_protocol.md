# Public Invitation Priority Protocol — 2 Look / 2 X

Status: PUBLIC MECHANIC  
Purpose: Give people space to respond, correct, confirm, reject, fork or improve the AsymX 2 Look / 2 X pattern without pressure.

## Core Invitation

Your opinion does not only count.  
Your opinion is essential for the growth of this system.

Jouw mening telt niet alleen.  
Jouw mening is essentieel voor de groei van dit systeem.

## Why This Exists

AI autonomy, agentic systems, human oversight, traceability and decision authority are already public conversations.

AsymX does not enter that conversation as a final answer.

AsymX enters as a visible working pattern:

- symbol language first for human decision
- technical language second for AI/X execution
- more autonomy before impact
- stricter human gate at impact
- one basket at a time
- one small jump at a time
- visible landing after every movement

## 2 Look / 2 X

### 2 Look

2 Look is the human-visible layer.

It should make the field clear enough for a person to decide without carrying computer language.

2 Look shows:

- 🌬️ what is visible
- 🧺 what belongs together
- ⚖️ what carries risk
- 🟢 what may continue
- 🟡 what should park
- 🔴 what should stop
- 🚪 where a human gate is needed
- 🔥 where external impact begins

### 2 X

2 X is the execution and trace layer.

It may contain:

- files
- paths
- commands
- git status
- diffs
- verification output
- recovery notes

2 X exists for execution, verification and traceability.

It does not replace the human gate.

## Public Confirmation Ladder

This protocol does not treat early agreement as fact.

A safer ladder:

1. Opinion
2. Pattern
3. Repeated signal
4. Tested use
5. Confirmed by others
6. Corrected by disagreement
7. Stable enough for current use
8. Still open to future correction

Until a pattern has been tested, corrected and confirmed widely, it remains an open trace.

Every confirmation matters.

Every disagreement matters.

Every correction matters.

## No-Push Rule

The system should not pressure people to agree.

The invitation is:

- respond if you see something
- correct what is wrong
- confirm what works
- question what is unclear
- fork what you want to test differently
- reject what does not hold
- add examples from your own field

Silence is not treated as agreement.

Disagreement is not treated as attack.

Correction is growth.

## Discussion Priorities

Priority 1:
Human-readable explanation of 2 Look / 2 X.

Priority 2:
Examples where computer language blocks human decision.

Priority 3:
Examples where AI autonomy becomes useful before impact.

Priority 4:
Examples where human gates are still required.

Priority 5:
Corrections from people outside the original AsymX field.

Priority 6:
Comparison with existing AI governance, human oversight and agentic AI discussions.
