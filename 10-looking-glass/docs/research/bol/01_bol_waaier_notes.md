## Role of the Excel waaier

The Excel waaier is the **leaf layer**: a place to capture **signals** that later become structured research material. It sits **above** raw browsing and **below** the official source-report anchors.

## What a “leaf” can be

A leaf can be, among other things:

- a **KPI** or number (only if source-tagged or marked **NEEDS SOURCE CHECK**);
- a **sentence** or paraphrase from a public page;
- a **public claim** as stated by bol or a named public document;
- a **tension** or **question** (explicitly labeled, not as fact);
- a **source reference** (URL + retrieval context → see `sources/` and `01_public_source_catalog.md`);
- an **open question** for later verification.

## Confidence

**Confidence** means **confidence in traceability to a named public source at a recorded time**, not ownership of “final” correctness. Low confidence = **NEEDS SOURCE CHECK** or **pending live verification** (especially for `B1b`).

## Instrument, not story

The waaier is a **working instrument**. The **main public story** for this invitation layer lives in `00_executive_summary.md` and the **official report layer** in `02_duurzaamheidsverslagen_2024_2025.md`. If the spreadsheet starts to read like a closed **dossier**, trim it back to **leaves** and **questions**.
