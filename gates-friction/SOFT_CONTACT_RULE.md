# AsymX Soft Contact Rule

## Core

Hoe levender het veld voelt,  
hoe zachter de buitenwereld benaderd moet worden.

Public contact begins with invitation, not pressure.

AsymX does not push a field open.  
AsymX offers a way to look.

We do not grab the hand for the dance.  
We reach out our hand.

The other side chooses whether to take it.

---

## Why this exists

A living field carries energy.

Too much pressure too early creates resistance.  
Too much certainty too early creates defence.  
Too much exposure too early creates friction.

The purpose of AsymX public movement is not to win, expose, accuse or convince.

The purpose is to make a tension visible in a way that both sides can still breathe.

Soft contact is not weakness.  
Soft contact is precision.

---

## The rule

When AsymX touches the public world:

- invite before explaining
- observe before naming
- show both sides before interpreting
- ask before entering deeper
- lower pressure when the field becomes more alive
- keep trace without exposing everything
- leave space for the other side to move freely

---

## Reiken, niet pakken

We reiken onze hand uit voor de dans.  
We trekken niemand de vloer op.

We laten zien dat bewegen veilig kan zijn.  
We bepalen niet dat de ander moet bewegen.

If the other side does not move, AsymX waits.  
If the other side moves, AsymX follows the shared rhythm.

The dance begins only when movement is mutual.

---

## Public language

Use:

> We noticed a tension.  
> Maybe this pattern is recognizable.  
> We place both sides next to each other.  
> No blame.  
> No final claim.  
> Only an invitation to look together.

Do not use:

> This proves failure.  
> They are doing it wrong.  
> This is the solution.  
> They must change.  
> We will show them.

---

## Pressure guide

| Public pressure | Unsafe movement | Soft contact movement |
|---|---|---|
| Title | “Proof of failure” | “Observation: tension between A and B” |
| Opening | “X is wrong” | “A pattern appears across contexts” |
| Friction | “This is unacceptable” | “Here, both sides seem to carry pressure” |
| Conclusion | “This must change” | “What would create more flow here?” |
| Contact | “We take the hand” | “We reach out a hand” |
| Tone | accusation | invitation |

## Human gate

Everything may be traceable internally.  
Not everything should be touched publicly.

The field may hold more than the outside world can carry.

Human gate decides the pressure of the touch.

When in doubt:

> lower pressure  
> soften language  
> keep both sides visible  
> invite instead of conclude  
> reiken, niet pakken

---

## Anchor

Hoe levender het veld voelt,  
hoe zachter de buitenwereld benaderd moet worden.

Reiken, niet pakken.  
Uitnodigen, niet trekken.  
Aanbieden, niet nemen.

The goal is not to win the friction.  
The goal is to make the dance visible.
